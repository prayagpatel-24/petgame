print(f"""
================ HOW TO PLAY: EVOLVING LIFE ================

EVOLVING LIFE is a console-based pet game where you take care of a pet and
manage the cost of caring for it using sparks (the in-game money).

The goal is to keep your pet happy, healthy, and not go broke on sparks.

---------------- STARTING A GAME ----------------

When you launch the program:

1. You will see:
   1. New Game
   2. Load Game

2. New Game:
   - Type 1
   - Enter your pet's name.
   - Enter the pet species (dog, cat, dragon, etc.).
   - The game starts on Day 1 with some basic supplies in your inventory.

3. Load Game:
   - Type 2
   - Choose a save slot.
   - If the slot has a pet, it will load that saved game.

---------------- MAIN STATS ----------------

Your pet has several important stats:

- Hunger: how hungry your pet is (high hunger is bad).
- Thirst: how thirsty your pet is (high thirst is bad).
- Energy: how tired your pet is (low energy is bad).
- Hygiene: how clean your pet is (low hygiene is bad).
- Happiness: how happy your pet is (low happiness can make them sad or leave).
- Health: overall health (if this gets too low, things go badly).
- Skill Points: points you can spend in the Skill Tree.

Your player stats:

- Day and Week: track how long you've been playing.
- Sparks: your money.
- Income: how much you've earned like through your weekly allowance.
- Expenses: how much you've spent on your pet.

Inventory:

- Food: used to feed your pet.
- Water: used to give your pet a drink.
- Soap: used to clean your pet.

---------------- MENUS AND CONTROLS ----------------

During the main game loop you will see a menu like this:

1. Feed
2. Water
3. Clean
4. Play
5. Inventory
6. Skill Tree
7. Store
8. Save
9. Next Day
10. Health Check
0. Quit

You always control the game by typing the number shown and pressing Enter.

If you type something that is not a valid menu number, the game tells you
it is not a correct option and you will be prompted to enter a valid option.

---------------- WHAT EACH ACTION DOES ----------------

1. Feed
   - Uses 1 Food from your inventory.
   - Lowers Hunger.
   - Increases Happiness a little.
   - If you run out of Food, you have to buy more from the store.

2. Water
   - Uses 1 Water from your inventory.
   - Lowers Thirst.
   - If you run out of Water, you need to buy more.

3. Clean
   - Uses 1 Soap from your inventory.
   - Increases Hygiene.
   - Also gives a small Happiness boost.
   - If Hygiene gets too low, it will hurt Health over time.

4. Play
   - Increases Happiness.
   - Uses up Energy.
   - Increases Hunger a bit.
   - Good for keeping your pet happy, but don't overdo it if they are exhausted.

5. Inventory
   - Shows how much Food, Water, and Soap you have left.

6. Skill Tree
   - Lets you spend Skill Points your pet has earned.
   - Skills:
     - Agility
     - Intelligence
     - Strength
     - Charm
   - When your pet has Skill Points, you can upgrade these skills to make your pet
     feel like it is developing over time.

7. Store
   - Where you buy Food, Water, and Soap.
   - Food costs 10 sparks.
   - Water costs 5 sparks.
   - Soap costs 8 sparks.
   - Every purchase adds to your expenses and subtracts sparks.

8. Save
   - Opens the save/load menu.
   - You can save to a slot, load from a slot, or clear a slot.
   - Saving ties your current game to a save slot (like slot1).
   - After that, the game can autosave changes to that slot.

9. Next Day
   - Moves the game forward to the next day.
   - A random event happens (good, neutral, or bad) which can change Happiness
     and a little Health.
   - Your pet may earn Skill Points based on Happiness.
   - Every 7 days, you get a weekly bonus of 20 sparks.
   - There is a simple care check: if Hunger or Thirst are too high or Hygiene is
     too low, your pet loses some Health.
   - At the end of the day you see a summary:
     - Day number
     - Current sparks
     - Total expenses
     - Updated stats

10. Health Check
   - Shows your pet's current Health and a short description:
     - Healthy and strong
     - Okay but could be better
     - Not doing well
   - From here you can:
     1. Visit the vet (costs 25 sparks)
        - Increases Health a lot.
        - Increases Happiness a bit.
        - Adds 25 sparks to your expenses.
     2. Go back
        - Do nothing if you can't afford it or don't want to.

0. Quit
   - Leaves the game loop.
   - If your game is connected to a save slot, the newest state can be saved
     so you can continue later.

---------------- MOOD AND RUNNING AWAY ----------------

Your pet's mood is based on Happiness:

- Very low Happiness: mood becomes "Ran Away" and the pet leaves.
- Low Happiness: "Sad".
- Medium Happiness: "Okay".
- Higher Happiness: "Happy".
- Very high Happiness: "Energetic".

If you never feed, water, clean, play, or rest your pet, Happiness and Health
will get worse. Eventually, the pet can run away and the game will end.

---------------- COST OF CARE AND MONEY ----------------

Sparks are the money you use to take care of your pet.

You SPEND sparks on:
- Food (10 per item)
- Water (5 per item)
- Soap (8 per item)
- Vet visits (25 each)

You EARN sparks from:
- Weekly bonus: every 7 days you get +20 sparks.

The game tracks:
- sparks: how much you currently have.
- expenses: total sparks you spent over time.
- income: total sparks you earned from bonuses.

At the end of each day you see how your money is doing. If you waste all
your sparks, it gets harder to buy food, water, soap, and vet care. This
shows the cost of caring for a real pet and why budgeting matters.

---------------- SAVING, LOADING, AND SLOTS ----------------

The game uses a file called saves.json to store your save data.

Save slots:
- slot1
- slot2
- slot3
- slot4

In the save/load menu:
- You see each slot with either:
  - EMPTY
  - Or the name of the pet in that slot and the day.
- You can choose a slot to save your current game.
- You can choose a slot to load an existing game.

Clearing a slot:
- There is an option to clear a slot.
- It will ask if you really want to clear it.
- If you say yes, that slot becomes EMPTY again.

------------------------ GOALS ------------------------

There isn't a real way to win but you can think of winning as:

- Keeping your pet alive, healthy, and happy for many days.
- Managing sparks so you don't go broke.
- Growing your pet's skills and watching its stats change over time.

However you can lose if your pet's Happiness gets so low that it runs away or
if its health goes too low and it dies.

---------------- Most Important Tips ----------------

- Don't ignore Hunger and Thirst. High values can slowly damage Health.
- Keep an eye on Hygiene; dirty pets are not happy or healthy.
- Use Rest when Energy is low so your pet doesn't get completely drained.
- Spend Skill Points whenever you have them so your pet feels like it is improving.
- Try not to burn through sparks too fast. Think before you buy everything.
- Use Health Check and the vet when Health drops too low.
- Remember to save your game to a slot so you can load it later.

If you balance care and money, your pet will stay around longer, be happier,
and you will get the most out of EVOLVING LIFE.
===========================================================
""")
